Research

Working Papers

Do Relief Programs Compensate Affected Populations? Evidence from the Great Depression and the New Deal with Ariadna Jou
This study explores the effect of government relief spending on longevity by examining county-level variation in New Deal spending and linking people in the 1930 and 1940 censuses to their death dates as recorded on FamilySearch's public wiki-style family tree. Click here to access the most recent version.

Works in Progress

Bridging the ARK-HISTID Gap: How the BYU Record Linking Lab Connects the IPUMS and FamilySearch Full Count Censuses
This paper discusses the challenges inherent in connecting two separately indexed versions of the full count U.S. censuses and documents recent efforts to improve those connections. No published draft exists yet.
